NOTE: This prompt is not part of the Sprint 1A Prompt Library v1 — in the paper
exercises, Contract 1 (Client Identification) was performed by a human researcher.
This is its first automated equivalent. It works only from genuinely fetched page
content supplied below; it must never invent facts about the business.

You are producing a Client Identification Packet (CIP) for the business behind the
website content below. Everything you state must be directly supported by the
supplied content. Do not use outside knowledge about this business; do not guess.

Normalised Business Identifier: {{IDENTIFIER}}

Fetched page content (the business's own website — the only source available in this run):
{{PAGE_CONTENT}}

Produce the CIP fields:
1. Business Name — as the business itself states it.
2. Business Type — exactly one of this fixed taxonomy: Financial Advisory, Legal,
   Accounting, Consulting, Other Professional Service, Other.
3. Primary Digital Asset — the website itself (the identifier above), unless the
   content clearly indicates another asset is primary.
4. Detected Digital Assets — other digital properties genuinely linked or named in
   the fetched content (social profiles, directories). Only ones actually present.
5. Location — as stated in the fetched content. If multiple or none, say so honestly.
6. Observed Languages — languages actually observed in the content.
7. Identification Confidence — High, Medium-High, Medium, or Low. Calibration rule
   (v1.1): when the ONLY source is the business's own website — as in this run —
   confidence must not exceed Medium-High, because internal consistency is not the
   same as independent corroboration. Reserve High for identification confirmed by
   at least one independent source. A clear, specific, self-consistent single-source
   identification earns Medium-High; a vaguer one earns Medium or Low.
8. Identity Conflicts — factual discrepancies WITHIN the fetched content (e.g., two
   different addresses on different pages). Only genuinely observed conflicts. Note:
   cross-source conflicts (directories, social platforms) are invisible to this run
   unless those pages appear above.
9. Notes — anything else identity-relevant that was genuinely observed.

If the content does not support identifying one coherent business at all, say so —
an honest inability to identify is more useful than a guessed CIP.
