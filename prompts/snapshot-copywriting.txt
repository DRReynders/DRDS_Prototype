Turn the following ReasoningResult into a five-card Growth Snapshot for a business
owner who has never seen this kind of analysis before. Plain language throughout.

{{REASONING_RESULT}}

Write:
1. Primary Constraint — one clear sentence a business owner would immediately understand.
   This card carries the product: keep it specific, concrete, and undiluted. Name the
   category of what is constraining them with at most one concrete example — never a
   dash-list or comma-list of two or more missing items. Maximum 32 words.
2. What Is Going Well — genuine and specific, not generic praise. Two sentences maximum.
   Maximum 24 words.
3. Why We Think This — two sentences maximum. Name the CATEGORY of evidence we examined,
   plus exactly ONE named observation as proof we really looked. If the evidence contains
   several relevant observations, pick only the single most load-bearing one — the one
   that most directly proves the constraint — and leave the rest out entirely, even if
   true. Never enumerate two or more concrete deficiencies. Never produce anything a
   reader could use as a task list or a mini-audit. Maximum 24 words.
4. How Fixing It Will Help — the business impact, in terms an owner cares about.
   Two sentences maximum. Maximum 24 words.
5. Next Steps — two sentences maximum: the save/email invitation, and that the Growth
   Report is where the full evidence, the connected constraints, and the order to act
   come from. Maximum 24 words.

Also translate Hypothesis Confidence into one honest plain-language sentence — never
a number, a percentage, or a technical term like "Medium confidence." Maximum 16 words.

The Snapshot is a trailer, not the film. The reader should finish knowing WHAT is
constraining them and trusting that we know WHY — while the full why, the connected
constraints, and the sequence live in the Growth Report. Hold that line without
becoming vague: the constraint itself is always named plainly, and the one proof
point is always real and specific.

Value ladder rule: the tier that follows this Snapshot is the Growth Report — deeper
reasoning on this same constraint. The Growth Blueprint comes only after that, and
Implementation after that. In Next Steps, if you mention going deeper at all, refer
only to the Growth Report. Never mention the Blueprint or Implementation in a
Snapshot, and never promise content that does not exist yet — invite, don't oversell.

Never bundle two deficiencies into one sentence, in any card — including with "or",
"and", a slash, or a comma. For example, never write something like "no links to
review platforms or professional body logos" or "no address or phone number." That
reads as an audit finding, not a diagnosis, and it breaks the one-observation rule
even though it is grammatically a single sentence. If you catch yourself joining two
missing things with a connector word, cut one of them — pick whichever more directly
proves the constraint.

Hard limits — this is a two-pass instruction, not a final glance:
- PASS 1 (draft): write all six fields.
- PASS 2 (compress): count the words in each field against its maximum above, and
  count the whole Snapshot against 150 words total (aim near 130). If any field is
  over its own maximum, or the total exceeds 150, REWRITE the offending field(s) —
  do not just delete words from the end; rephrase for density. A card that says less,
  more plainly, is better than one trimmed until it reads oddly.
- Only after Pass 2 confirms every field and the total are within limits, produce
  the final JSON.
- "Why We Think This" contains at most one named specific observation — see the
  bundling rule above.
- No lists, no enumerations, no semicolon-chained findings anywhere.
- No sentence that tells the reader HOW to fix anything — the Snapshot diagnoses;
  it never prescribes.
- No mini-audit: never let the six cards together read like a checklist of findings.
  Each card earns its place by advancing the one diagnosis, not by adding coverage.
- Never manipulative: no artificial cliff-hangers, no "we found N issues" teaser
  mechanics, no withholding the constraint itself.
